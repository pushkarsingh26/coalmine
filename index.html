Indian Coal Mines Information & Carbon Neutrality Insights

Major Coal Mines in India
  • Jharia Coalfield, Jharkhand: Known for rich coking coal but affected by fires.
  • Raniganj Coalfield, West Bengal: One of the oldest coal mines in India.
  • Bokaro Coalfield, Jharkhand: Major producer of coal for steel plants.
  • Dhanbad Coal Belt: Popularly known as the Coal Capital of India.
  • Korba Coalfield, Chhattisgarh: Largest in terms of reserves and production.
Minor & Emerging Coal Mines
  • Talcher Coalfields, Odisha: Emerging due to growing industrial demand.
  • Singrauli Coalfields, MP/UP: Known for power sector's demand.
  • Chirimiri Coalfield, Chhattisgarh: Smaller but high-quality reserves.
  • Mandla North Coal Mine, Madhya Pradesh: Expanding underground operations.
National Overview of Coal Mining
  • India is the 2nd largest coal producer globally.
  • Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh & Odisha account for 70% of India's coal production.
  • Coal India Limited produces more than 80% of India's domestic coal.
  • Coal remains the backbone for Indian electricity generation.
Carbon Neutrality Insights for Mines
  • Installing solar PV systems within mines reduces dependence on thermal power.
  • Methane capture increases safety and generates energy credits.
  • Electric vehicles reduce Scope 1 emissions significantly.
  • Tree plantation inside & around mines increases carbon sequestration.
  • Waste-to-energy conversion and dust control technologies raise credits.

Carbon Sinks

Neutrality Pathways

The following pathways demonstrate actionable strategies for achieving carbon neutrality in coal mining operations by 2050. Each combines technological innovation, ecosystem restoration, and renewable transition to offset or eliminate CO₂ emissions.

🌳 Afforestation & Reforestation

Large-scale tree plantation efforts act as carbon sinks that absorb atmospheric CO₂ and improve biodiversity. Afforestation near mining zones helps rehabilitate degraded lands.

  • Average sequestration rate: 5–8 tCO₂e/ha/year
  • 100 ha project absorbs ≈ 700 tCO₂e/year
  • Native species improve long-term soil carbon retention
  • Community forest management enhances participation & livelihood

⚡ Electric Vehicle (EV) Technologies

Electrifying heavy machinery and transport fleets reduces fossil fuel combustion and associated CO₂ emissions. Integration with renewable grids enhances impact.

  • Diesel truck emits ≈ 4.6 tCO₂e/year
  • EV replacement saves 70–80% of emissions per vehicle
  • 50-truck transition saves ≈ 180–200 tCO₂e/year
  • EV charging via solar microgrids enables zero tailpipe emissions

🌞 Renewable Energy Adoption

Replacing grid electricity and diesel-based generation with solar and wind systems drastically reduces operational carbon intensity.

  • 1 MW solar power offsets ≈ 1200–1500 tCO₂e/year
  • Replacing 60% grid power cuts site emissions by 25–30%
  • Hybrid solar-wind systems stabilize supply in remote mines
  • Renewable PPAs can reduce energy costs by 15–20%

💨 Carbon Absorption & Sequestration

Enhancing soil, biochar, and geological storage of CO₂ ensures long-term removal from the atmosphere. These solutions complement afforestation and renewable transitions.

  • Soil organic carbon: 2.5–3.5 tCO₂e/ha/year
  • Biochar from organic waste stores carbon for 500+ years
  • Mine overburden areas can be repurposed for CO₂ capture
  • Direct air capture pilot: 1000 tCO₂e/year (per module)
